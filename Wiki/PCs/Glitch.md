## Description

Petra is the kind of teenager who has a file. Possibly several files, at
several agencies, none of which fully agree on what she actually did. She
has strong opinions about authority, a reflexive need to poke at anything
that looks like a rule, and the technical skills to make poking at rules
genuinely consequential. Her hero name was self-assigned, taken from the
error message that appeared on a government server the first time she
accidentally crashed it with her powers.

She keeps her cape persona deliberately chaotic — part performance, part
armor. The hero underneath the rebel act is more competent, more careful,
and more invested than Glitch ever lets on publicly. Whether that hero
is the real Petra or just another mask is a question she hasn't answered
for herself yet.

## Background

Petra has been in trouble with authorities for as long as she can remember,
initially for conventional hacking and later for things that were harder
to classify once her powers came online. She's learned that the best way
to avoid being taken seriously as a threat is to be taken seriously as
an annoyance instead. This strategy has worked moderately well and
created several enemies who underestimate her.

She keeps trying to impress Vigil, which she would never admit to doing
and which she suspects is obvious to everyone. She and Cinder pulled off
an impressively ill-advised stunt together before the team officially
formed — the details of which neither of them has fully disclosed.
She is acutely aware that Cinder hurt her once. She has complicated
feelings about whether she's forgiven him.

## Moves

**Mary Contrary** — When someone tries to pierce Glitch's mask, comfort
or support her, or provoke her, she can interfere. Roll +Superior. Hit:
they take -2 on their roll. 10+: also take Influence over them OR clear
a condition. Miss: they get a 10+ regardless, and Glitch marks a
condition of their choice.

**I don't care what you think!** — Whenever Glitch rejects others'
Influence, add +2 to the roll.

**Troublemaker** — When Glitch helps a teammate through destructive,
criminal, or rule-breaking actions, she can give them +2 instead of +1
when spending a Team.

*Delinquent team moves:*
- **Triumphant celebration:** Ask the teammate if they think she's cool.
  Yes → give them Influence and take Influence over them. No → mark a
  condition or spurn them immediately. Either way (if teammate): add 1 Team.
- **Vulnerability/weakness:** Give them Influence and ask who they'd like
  her to be. Mark potential if she shows them that person.
  (If teammate: add 1 Team regardless.)

## Special Features

**The rebel act vs. the hero underneath**
Glitch's core tension is the Delinquent's dramatic question made concrete:
the chaos persona is load-bearing. It keeps people from taking her
seriously as a threat, keeps authority at arm's length, and keeps her
from having to commit to being the hero she might actually be. When the
act slips — when she chooses the team over the bit — it should land.

**I don't care what you think! — note on mechanics**
With Superior at +2, Glitch is already difficult to move emotionally.
This move makes rejecting Influence a viable active choice rather than
a last resort. She will use it. This should sometimes have costs.

**Cypher's watch order**
Vigil was asked by Cypher to keep an eye on Glitch. Glitch does not
know this. Whether she would care, and what she would do if she found
out, are open questions.

**The Cinder wound**
Cinder hurt Glitch when he lost control of his powers. The nature and
severity of this incident is left for play to establish. What matters
mechanically: it is in the room every time they interact, and the
triumphant celebration question — *do you think she's cool?* — is
complicated by it.
