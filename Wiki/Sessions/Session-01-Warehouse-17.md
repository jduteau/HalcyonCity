# Session 1 — Warehouse 17

## Summary

On the team's first operational night, a thermal anomaly alert at Warehouse 17 on Dock Street led them to Elena Reyes — a physics grad student who had just manifested uncontrolled light/heat powers after being attacked by Pinnacle Solutions extraction operatives. Cinder talked her down and then flew her to safety when she went critical, detonating a massive energy burst above the waterfront. Back at base, Vigil's research uncovered Pinnacle Solutions' roots in Prometheus Labs — a shuttered human experimentation program designed to identify future metahumans before they manifested. A mysterious contact called Subject 17 then warned that Pinnacle is running a city-wide sweep, naming three more targets. The session ended with the team split across the city racing to intercept.

## Narrative

The night was supposed to be quiet. Cypher had been gone three days — longer than usual — and the three of them were running training routines and equipment checks in the base's blue-white monitor light when the alert fired.

Thermal bloom at Warehouse 17, Dock Street. Heat signature unlike combustion — localized, mobile, and climbing. Three unmarked vans in the lot. The local homeless population had vanished.

Vigil chose the tunnel route for the element of surprise. Twelve minutes underground, moving in formation, until the sounds from above changed: metal screaming, then a thump that vibrated the concrete underfoot. They took the service ladder up and emerged in the alley beside the warehouse to find a section of brick wall melted like wax — and through the gap, a figure standing in pulsing white-gold light surrounded by six unconscious tactical operatives.

She was twenty-two years old and terrified. Elena Reyes, physics grad student, pajamas scorched, parents three years dead, no family, and something burning inside her that she couldn't stop and didn't understand. The van crews had come for her in her sleep; she'd fought back without knowing how.

Cinder stepped forward and showed her his fire. *I know what this feels like. You're in control, even when it doesn't feel like it.* For a moment it almost worked. Then Elena's exhaustion overtook her will, the light surged, and the warehouse became an oven. Vigil ordered Cinder to take her up and get her out. He grabbed her, flew through the melted gap, and rocketed two hundred meters into the night sky. The energy discharge lit up the waterfront — a silent sphere of white fire that blew out windows across the district — and then faded. Cinder came down in the alley with his flames guttered and Elena unconscious in his arms.

Back at base, Sam collapsed on the couch while Vigil and Petra started digging. Elena — when she came around — pieced together the shape of it faster than most: the bloodwork at a university wellness screening three weeks ago had been predictive biomarker testing. Someone had screened her before she even had powers.

Petra hit Pinnacle Solutions' servers hard and fast. Too hard, too fast. Military-grade ICE caught the trace. She killed it before they could follow it home, but they know someone is looking. She took the Guilty condition and hasn't quite forgiven herself.

Vigil's research thread ran deeper. Marcus Webb, Pinnacle's CEO, had previously run Prometheus Labs — a research division shut down eight years ago under allegations of unethical human experimentation. One leaked internal memo, buried in Cypher's archives, revealed the research focus: *identification and activation of latent metahuman genetic markers.* They weren't looking for people who had powers. They were looking for people who would.

Then the base's secure channel chimed. Old encryption — Cypher's protocols from years ago. A voice modulator, bouncing through seven proxies. The caller identified themselves as Subject 17: a former Prometheus Labs test subject who had survived whatever Webb did to them. They had intel. Pinnacle was running a city-wide sweep tonight, accelerated by Glitch's intrusion. Three targets identified: Marcus Chen (19, electrokinetic, Eastside bodega), Deshawn Williams (16, phasing, Northgate High area), Lily Vasquez (14, pre-manifestation, Miller Street group home). The caller closed with a message for Cypher: *the debt isn't settled yet.*

Less than forty minutes to the first extraction team's arrival. Vigil split the team: Cinder to Miller Street for Lily, Vigil to Northgate for Deshawn, Glitch to the Eastside bodega for Marcus — her first solo field operation, running on nerve and the conviction that she's always faster and smarter. Elena stayed at base on comms.

The session ended with the team in motion across a city that doesn't yet know what's being hunted inside it.
