# Gold Generation

Heroes born from the 1920s to the 1940s. The first with publicly recorded superhumans acting in their own personas.

## Overview

[[Wiki/NPCs/Flying-Freedom]] started it all, but she was far from the last of her generation. Champion, the Haunt, Golden Girl, Bryce Brilliant, the Cast-Iron Man — these were the heroes of the era.

This generation pulled America out of the Depression. They sent supers into World War II. They created the modern concept of the superhero.

## Enemies and Threats

Gold Generation villains were often powerful criminals, unscrupulous corporate fatcats, or corrupt politicians — with occasional larger threats like the Gorgomoth (a gigantic minotaur) or evil geniuses like [[Wiki/NPCs/Captain-Coldheart]]. Countless non-violent costumed criminals stole artifacts and played pranks on public officials.

## Social Context

Women and minority heroes were laughed at, degraded, and ignored — they fought for every ounce of respect. The entire city was filled with the prejudice of the era. Champion's struggle as a homosexual man and public figure in the 1940s illustrates that even "simpler" heroic conflicts coexisted with profound personal ones.

## Current Status

Most Gold Generation heroes are either gone or retired. Those still active are either trying to reclaim lost glory (e.g., the Scarlet Songbird wants nothing more than to be a relevant supercriminal again) or are the most powerful and enduring members of the generation.

## Relevance to Campaign

The Gold Generation represents the origin point of Halcyon's superheroic culture. Their noble ideals — and their all-too-easy oversights — weigh on the Modern Generation PCs.
