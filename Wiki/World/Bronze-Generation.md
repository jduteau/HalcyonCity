# Bronze Generation

Heroes born from the 1970s to the 1980s. Defined by a single inciting tragedy — and a generation-wide reckoning with the failures of those who came before.

## The Death of Sam Reed

The accepted transition point from Silver to Bronze: Quintessence (Niall Collins), a younger hero acting in the Silver Generation's style, was targeted by the telepath Psilence. Psilence publicly abducted Sam Reed — Niall's best friend — and threatened him to get to Quintessence. During their battle, Psilence knocked Sam from the top of the Colossus Building in downtown Halcyon. Quintessence dove down the side of the building after him.

Medical examiners couldn't confirm the cause of Sam's death. He may have been dead before Psilence cast him off, struck the building on the way down, or died from the force of Quintessence catching him. Either way, **Sam Reed died for being friends with Niall Collins.**

## Overview

The ripple of Sam Reed's death forced a wave of introspection on the younger generation. They saw that the Silver Generation was often more focused on grand gestures than real change — and still clung to power, leaving no room for new heroes.

New heroes rose with new ideas: the sword-wielding vigilante Guillotine, the politically active Blue Eagle, the dimension-hopping Dr. Sharon Supreme, the government spy Soar (Agent Caldwell Wing).

## Character

The Bronze Generation was the first to truly doubt superheroism as it had existed. They opened doors to different ways of being heroes — even if many of those doors led to dark places. They took on social issues the prior generations had abandoned. They were darker, more exploratory, more cynical.

## Current Status

Very much present and active today. Though the Silver Generation still holds most positions of power, the Bronze Generation has found its own footing and is ready to seize the reins.

## Relevance to Campaign

Bronze Generation heroes are the immediate predecessors — and sometimes direct mentors — of the Modern Generation PCs. Their frustration at the system and their cynicism are a visible cautionary tale.
