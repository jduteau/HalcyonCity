# Superhero Generations

Halcyon City has seen four relatively distinct generations of superheroes over roughly 80 years. The dividing lines aren't explicit — each generation bleeds into the next — but each developed its own clear voice.

## The Four Generations

| Generation | Era | Defining Character |
|------------|-----|--------------------|
| [[Wiki/World/Gold-Generation]] | 1920s–1940s | First public heroes; mundane crime; WWII |
| [[Wiki/World/Silver-Generation]] | 1950s–1960s | Explosive power; cosmic threats; super teams |
| [[Wiki/World/Bronze-Generation]] | 1970s–1980s | Cynicism; social critique; darker methods |
| [[Wiki/World/Modern-Generation]] | 1990s–today | Aware of all prior history; still finding identity |

## Current State

All three prior generations are still present and active to varying degrees. The Silver Generation holds most positions of power but is moving toward retirement. The Bronze Generation has found its own footing and is ready to seize the reins. The Modern Generation — including the young heroes of the campaign — is ascending.

## Relevance to Campaign

The PCs are the Modern Generation. They see clearly:
- The Gold Generation's nobility, and its all-too-easy oversight
- The Silver Generation's power and success, and its domineering attitudes
- The Bronze Generation's frustration and search for new alternatives, and its ultimate failure to change anything significantly

All of that history weighs on them as they decide who they want to be.
