# Superhero Generations

Halcyon City's superheroic history is divided into four generations. The dividing lines are fuzzy — each generation bleeds into the next — but each developed a distinct voice, aesthetic, and set of concerns.

The generational breakdown reflects changes in power levels, social consciousness, and the relationship between heroes and the city they protect.

## The Four Generations

- [[Wiki/Lore/Gold-Generation]] — Born 1920s–1940s. First public heroes. Mundane threats, nobility, direct civic impact.
- [[Wiki/Lore/Silver-Generation]] — Born 1950s–1960s. Cosmic-scale power and threats. Team formation. World-ending battles.
- [[Wiki/Lore/Bronze-Generation]] — Born 1970s–1980s. Moral ambiguity, cynicism, reclaimed social consciousness.
- [[Wiki/Lore/Modern-Generation]] — Born 1990s–today. Still finding their name and identity.

## The Transition Moments

- **Gold → Silver**: An unexplained surge in power levels during the 1950s. The first hero to clearly embody this shift was [[Wiki/NPCs/Silver-Savior]].
- **Silver → Bronze**: The death of Sam Reed, thrown from the Colossus Building by Psilence and killed — possibly by the force of Quintessence catching him. The Bronze Generation grew up in the shadow of this failure.
- **Bronze → Modern**: No single event. The Modern Generation simply arrived into a world that had already normalized superhumans.

## Current State

All four generations coexist today. The Silver Generation still holds significant institutional power; the Bronze Generation is positioned to inherit those roles as Silver heroes retire; the Modern Generation is watched closely by everyone else, with a mix of hope and trepidation.
