# Bronze Generation

*Heroes born from the 1970s to the 1980s.* The first generation to truly question superheroism as it had existed. Darker, more morally complex, and more socially engaged than the [[Wiki/Lore/Silver-Generation]] they grew up under.

## The Catalyst: The Death of Sam Reed

The transition from Silver to Bronze is traced to a single incident. Quintessence (secretly Niall Collins) was attacked by his foe Psilence, a telepath who publicly abducted Niall's best friend Sam Reed and threw him from the top of the Colossus Building in downtown [[Wiki/World/Halcyon-City]].

Quintessence dove after Sam. Medical examiners could not say with certainty what killed him — it may have been Psilence's throw, impact with the building, or the force of Quintessence catching him. Either way: Sam Reed died for being friends with a superhero.

This forced a wave of introspection. The younger generation saw that the Silver Generation had become more focused on grand gestures than real change — and still clung to power, leaving no room for new voices.

## Character & Approach

The Bronze Generation opened new doors — some dark:
- Guillotine: sword-wielding, murderous vigilante
- Blue Eagle: patriotic and overtly political
- Dr. Sharon Supreme: dimension-hopping adventurer
- Soar (Agent Caldwell Wing): super-powered government spy

They reclaimed social issues the Silver Generation had abandoned, questioned the entire system, and carved out their own identity as a darker, more exploratory generation.

## Today

The Bronze Generation is very much active. While the Silver Generation still holds institutional power, the Bronze Generation has found its own sources of authority and influence. They are positioned to seize the reins as Silver heroes move into retirement.
