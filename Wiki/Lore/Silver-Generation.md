# Silver Generation

*Heroes born from the 1950s to the 1960s.* Defined by a sudden, unexplained surge in power levels. The Silver Generation fought world-ending threats and largely abandoned mundane social issues in favor of cosmic struggles.

## Origin

In the 1950s, superpowers became more powerful for reasons unknown. The first widely acknowledged representative was [[Wiki/NPCs/Silver-Savior]] — nigh-invulnerable, faster than ever seen, wielder of the Silver Spark.

## Character & Threats

Silver Generation heroes didn't contend with costumed thieves — they fought:
- Doctor Infinity (an all-powerful time-altering android)
- The Jabberwock (a monster risen from fiction to convert reality into words)
- Planet-enders, dimension destroyers

Battles leveled whole city blocks, changed the sky's color for days, left ozone and smoke across the city. [[Wiki/World/Halcyon-City]] became expert at rebuilding at incredible speed.

## Team Formation

The scale of threats drove heroes to organize. This generation spawned major super teams: the Exemplars, the Silver Saviors, the Amazing Eight. Villains followed suit with their own alliances.

## Social Trajectory

Early on, the Silver Generation made genuine strides on civil rights — a major public push for equal rights in Halcyon and America. Some took overtly political roles.

As their power grew, their focus shifted upward. By their prime, most had abandoned struggles against social issues, criminals, and corrupt politicians in favor of ever-greater cosmic threats. There were exceptions, but this shift defined the generation.

## Today

The Silver Generation is still largely present and in power. They are reaching the end of their era and many are looking toward retirement. Many Silver heroes want to find successors they can shape into new versions of themselves — ensuring continuity on their own terms. This creates friction with younger generations.

See [[Wiki/Lore/Superhero-Generations]] for context. The Bronze Generation's rise was partly a reaction to the Silver Generation's failures.
