# Gold Generation

*Heroes born from the 1920s to the 1940s.* The first publicly recorded generation of superhumans in [[Wiki/World/Halcyon-City]].

## Origin

[[Wiki/NPCs/Flying-Freedom]] (Maggie MacIntyre) started it all — the first figure to whom the term "superhero" could reasonably be applied. She inspired Champion and the Haunt, who carried on after her death. The Gold Generation created the very notion of the modern superhero.

## Character & Threats

Gold Generation heroes had a direct and real effect on mundane city life. Their opponents were often powerful criminals, unscrupulous corporate fatcats, or corrupt politicians — not cosmic entities.

Occasional large threats appeared (Gorgomoth, a gigantic minotaur; Captain Coldheart and his aerial doomcraft), but these were rare. The city was not under constant existential threat.

## Notable Heroes

- [[Wiki/NPCs/Flying-Freedom]] — Maggie MacIntyre; first public superhero; died saving Halcyon from Captain Coldheart
- Champion — one of the first inspired by Flying Freedom; homosexual man fighting for respect in the 1940s
- The Haunt
- Golden Girl
- Bryce Brilliant
- The Cast-Iron Man
- The Scarlet Songbird (villain — now wants to reclaim relevance)

## Social Context

Women and minority heroes faced constant dismissal, degradation, and prejudice — fighting for every ounce of respect. Champion's struggle as a homosexual public figure in the 1940s exemplifies the human complexity beneath the heroics.

## Today

Most Gold Generation heroes are gone or retired. Those still active are either:
- Trying to reclaim lost relevance (e.g., the Scarlet Songbird)
- Among the most powerful and enduring figures of any generation
